What Makun Is Like

Makun is where Sagamu is growing. The stretch of land running along the expressway corridor from the interchange toward Makun junction has seen more new construction, new businesses, and new arrivals in the last five years than any other part of the LGA. It is the part of Sagamu that feels like it is being built in real time.

The expressway access defines everything about Makun. Lagos is 60 kilometres away and reachable in under an hour when the road is clear. Ibadan is a similar distance in the other direction. That positioning makes Makun an attractive base for professionals who need to commute or travel regularly, which is why a large proportion of the relocated professionals who arrive in Sagamu end up settling here.

Restaurants, hotels, pharmacies, supermarkets, and service businesses have opened along the Makun Road corridor at a steady pace. It is not yet the density you would find in Lekki or GRA Benin, but the trajectory is clear. The businesses that are here are serving a population that has money and expectations, and the quality reflects that.

Housing ranges from gated estate apartments to standalone residential compounds. The newer developments on the Makun corridor side offer facilities that older Sagamu housing stock does not: estate security, maintained roads, water supply, and reliable power (some with dedicated generators). These come at a premium but the gap with what you would pay for equivalent accommodation in Lagos is still significant.

What You Will Find Here

The Junction Kitchen, one of Sagamu's better restaurants, is in Makun. Several pharmacies. A few banks with functional ATMs. Fuel stations. A growing number of convenience stores. Evening entertainment options are limited but developing.

Schools in Makun include a mix of private schools that cater to the professional resident population. Most relocated families with children find adequate schooling options within Makun or a short drive from it. Public primary schools are accessible across the area.
