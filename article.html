Sagamu Is Moving: What You Need to Know Right Now

by The Sagamu.ng Team | June 2025 | 7 min read

Sagamu is no longer just a junction on the Lagos-Ibadan Expressway. The city is building something. Businesses are opening, a world-class teaching hospital is expanding its services, two football clubs are putting Remo on the national map, and schools across the LGA are investing in new facilities. This is your guide to all of it.

For decades, Sagamu was seen as a town you passed through on the way to Lagos or Ibadan. The interchange was famous, the name was familiar, but what actually happened inside the town was invisible to anyone who did not live there. That is changing.

The Sagamu-Ore road feeds the largest corridor of industrial activity in southwest Nigeria outside Lagos. Sagamu sits at its entry point.

The Industrial Story Nobody Is Telling

Lafarge Africa has been operating a major plant near Sagamu for years. Coleman Wires and Cables is here. Alufoil Products is here. These are not small operations. They employ hundreds of people, many of whom have relocated from Lagos, Ibadan, and across Nigeria. Every new employee arrival is a household looking for schools, clinics, food, weekend activities, and a community. Sagamu.ng exists to serve that household.

Less visibly, Sagamu sits at the origin point of one of Nigeria's more interesting informal supply chains. Timber from Taraba and the middle belt arrives here, gets containerised, and moves onward to ports for export. Agro-processing operations in the LGA receive raw materials from Ogun State's rural hinterland and ship finished goods outward. The road infrastructure that makes Sagamu awkward to live in also makes it commercially indispensable.

OOUTH: More Than a Referral Hospital

Olabisi Onabanjo University Teaching Hospital is the foremost tertiary health institution in Ogun State, and one of the most capable in southwest Nigeria. It is not a referral-only facility. Walk-in outpatient registration is available. The 24-hour Accident and Emergency department is fully staffed. Specialist departments cover the full range of medical disciplines from internal medicine to burns and plastic surgery.

What most newcomers do not realise is that OOUTH is expanding. New units are being commissioned. The hospital attracts visiting consultants from Lagos teaching hospitals. For families relocating to the Sagamu area, this is genuinely good news: healthcare at a standard you would expect in a much larger city is available within the town.

Football and Civic Pride

Remo Stars FC is one of the best-run clubs in the Nigeria Premier Football League. Their stadium in Ikenne-Remo has become a genuine matchday destination. The youth development programme is producing players that attract attention from bigger clubs. Ebedei FC, at the grassroots level, keeps competitive football alive in the LGA for young players who are not yet on the professional radar.

This matters beyond sport. Cities with well-run football clubs have a different energy. Matchday brings people together across class and neighbourhood lines. Remo Stars has given Sagamu a reason for civic pride at a moment when the town needs one.

Schools: Investing in the Next Generation

Mayflower School in nearby Ikenne-Remo, founded by Tai Solarin, remains one of the most recognised boarding schools in southwest Nigeria. Remo Secondary School carries decades of academic tradition and an alumni network that spans every profession in the country. The state government has been running infrastructure programmes across primary schools in the LGA: new classrooms, laboratory equipment, sanitation blocks.

Private schools in Sagamu are investing in facilities at a pace that suggests confidence in the town's growth. New laboratory blocks, sports facilities, and digital learning resources are appearing. This is a function of the incoming professional class: parents who have moved from Lagos expect Lagos-standard education and the schools are responding.

What to Watch

The Sagamu-Ore road rehabilitation, long delayed, is now scheduled. When that road improves, the commercial case for Sagamu strengthens further. New residential estates are under development in the Makun corridor. The Sagamu interchange itself is being studied for upgrades.

None of this is guaranteed. Infrastructure timelines in Nigeria are what they are. But the trajectory is clear, and the fundamentals are real. Sagamu.ng will track all of it.
