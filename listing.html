About The Junction Kitchen

Proper sit-down dining near the Sagamu interchange. The Junction Kitchen is the kind of place that works equally well for a business lunch or a Saturday family dinner — the menu moves between continental dishes and solid Nigerian staples without doing either poorly.

The pepper soup is particularly good. The kitchen runs a set daily menu with specials that rotate, which keeps regulars coming back. Service is attentive without being intrusive. The space seats around 60 indoors with a covered outdoor section that catches the evening breeze.

It is one of the more reliable spots in Sagamu for entertaining visitors from out of town. Air conditioning, clean facilities, and a kitchen that takes presentation seriously. The price point is mid-range for Sagamu, which means it is affordable by Lagos standards but a noticeable step up from the roadside alternatives.

Recommended: the catfish pepper soup, the jollof rice with fried plantain, and the fresh juice combinations. Avoid the busy Friday and Saturday lunchtime periods if you are not willing to wait — the place fills up.
